Evergreenprinciples I keep returning to
  • 01Before you build something, ask if it needs to exist at all.
  • 02If you can remove it and nothing breaks, remove it.
  • 03A good tool stays out of your way. The best one, you forget you're using.
  • 04If you need a manual to use it, the design is making you do its job.
  • 05Every feature you add is a promise to keep it working — forever.
  • 06Don't start from what everyone else built. Start from the problem in front of you.
  • 07Count the time a tool costs you, not just the time it saves.
  • 08Anyone can add. The hard part is knowing what to leave out.
  • 09Most "must-have" features are just habits no one ever questioned.
  • 10When something feels confusing, it's not you. It's the design.
  • 11Simple isn't less. It's everything that matters and nothing that doesn't.
  • 12A tool should help you finish — not keep you coming back.
  • 13Ask one thing: did this actually make life easier? If not, cut it.
