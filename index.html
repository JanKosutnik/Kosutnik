principles (13)
  1. 01Before you build something, ask if it needs to exist at all.
  2. 02If you can remove it and nothing breaks, remove it.
  3. 03A good tool stays out of your way. The best one, you forget you're using.
  4. 04If you need a manual to use it, the design is making you do its job.
  5. 05Every feature you add is a promise to keep it working — forever.
  6. 06Don't start from what everyone else built. Start from the problem in front of you.
  7. 07Count the time a tool costs you, not just the time it saves.
  8. 08Anyone can add. The hard part is knowing what to leave out.
  9. 09Most "must-have" features are just habits no one ever questioned.
  10. 10When something feels confusing, it's not you. It's the design.
  11. 11Simple isn't less. It's everything that matters and nothing that doesn't.
  12. 12A tool should help you finish — not keep you coming back.
  13. 13Ask one thing: did this actually make life easier? If not, cut it.
